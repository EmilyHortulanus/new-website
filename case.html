In July 2016 I joined the Nodum team as a UX graduate intern from the University of Applied Sciences Utrecht (Hogeschool Utrecht), to help Nodum grow their user-base.

Nodum

Nodum — meaning ‘knot’ in Latin — is a startup which aims to be the ideal browser-based development environment with a diverse palette of integrated features. Nodum enables its users to easily create complex and API-rich applications, without the need for extensive back-end development knowledge.

The challenge

Nodum launched its initial website at the start of April 2016 to showcase their product and generate new users. Their website targeted four different target audiences. But after three months, new sign-ups were lower than aspired and the Nodum team decided that it was time for change. New research and market exploration hinted at a more specific target audience that would provide a better market-fit for the product. This new target audience would be defined as ‘the freelance front-end developer’.

But questions arose: how do you effectively communicate the value of the product with its many complicated and complex features? And how do you trigger website visitors to sign-up for the product? After an initial brief the following design challenge was formulated:

“How can Nodum increase website sign-ups amongst freelance front-end developers?”.
Interviews, ideation, prototyping, user testing.

A solution

Applying an iterative design thinking process, I conceptualized, prototyped and tested a new ‘persuasive website’. In the process I deployed insights gained from extensive user-research and a theoretical framework grounded in persuasive design, consumer psychology and technology.

Product website with the right persuasive triggers and a clear value proposition.
Flipping the funnel

The final concept, which was the end result of three iterations, makes use of a ‘funnel flip’ method where users can experience the product before signing-up. In the experience a multitude of persuasive elements are applied to make users more susceptible to motivational triggers.

Create a ‘todo app’ with Nodum, before you sign-up!

The ‘funnel flip’ method gives prospects the opportunity to experience that first ‘quick win’ through the website — as if they were already using the product. Then, when motivation is sparked and prospects are most likely to invest, they are guided towards signing-up with persuasive triggers which embody aspired user goals.

The implementation of this concept lead to a significant 22% increase in user sign-ups. I graduated with this project scoring a 9 out of 10.
